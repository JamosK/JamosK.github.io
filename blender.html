    Small Projects
    There are many individual renders I have made, and here is every single notable one
    2022/7/8 "Robot Tonk"
    At the time of this I was playing through Elden Ring, which has hundreds of unique enemy designs. And I wanted to try making a Elden Ring like enemy. It turns out, industry standard models are way out of my skill. As the enemy I made even when being very basic looks way lower quality then anything in Elden Ring. The biggest issue with it is the base surface is too flat, as I should have done some sort of sculpting. From this project I also learned about blending materials, which it turns out blender has poor support for and I had to build a node tree and make image masks myself.
    2022/7/14 "Realistic Object"
    At somepoint I wanted to make a render that looked realistic, using all the advanced lighting and stuff. So I modeled this box which required thinking about the PBR material settings more then ever before. Like the small finger prints are part of the roughness channel, As in the real world finger print oil would effect the roughness. While in the past I probably would have put the finger print oil in the albedo channel. And from the direction my modeling takes, this may the most realistic thing I ever rendered.
    2022/7/22 "Reflective Room"
    I learning about blenders Ray Tracing, and realized I could make infinity mirrors that would be impossible in real life. So after making a very reflective material, and changing the max number of allowed reflections, I was able to render the infinity mirror room.
    2022/12/24 "Bottle"
    I have zero memory of this. But appears I got more practice painting textures by hand.
    2023/3/20 "Windows Background 1"
    I wanted to personalize my computer, so I made a windows background. I used weight paints to control where the trees where, which may have been the first time I did that. As per usual the materials are the best looking part of it, with the models being passable. And the sky is just a image I composited in.
    2023/7/28 "Windows Background 2"
    The second windows background I made is very different. I had gotten quite interested into blenders node based materials. So I decided to make 45 of those materials for 1 image. And I really had to get creative to make more materials, like I discovered a number of new techniques to generate unique textures. Blenders nodes aren't really intended for generating texture like this, but I managed to push them to their limits.
    2023/9/22 "Baked Lighting Experiments"
    This is from one time I tried out rendering with baked lighting. The idea of baked lighting is instead of doing real time ray traced mathematics to find what a scene looks like, you do that math and save the result in textures. And then just re-render the scene using those textures. The result I got was ok, you can see some issues at the borders of objects. And it appears I didn't use a Denoiser, maybe this is because I thought it looked cool. I also really faced the reality of the massive texture size necessary for this rendering to look good. I also learned your lighting has to be very diffuse for the result to look passable.
    2023/6/11 "Style Scene"
    I wanted to make a non-realistic rendered scene, like stylized rendering. And I made something, but you don't need a design degree to say this image looks garbage. There ain't no color palette, no composition, no lighting, and most importantly no style.
    2024/1/26 "Stylized Street Scene"
    But multiple months later I returned, motivated to make style. Because I had gained a fragment of knowledge about stylized rendering. That good stylized rendering often modifies realistic rendering, instead of going on a completely new path. So I modified realistic rendering until it looked like this. And it came out baked.
    I also made a explanation of the node tree behind the shader for a friend.
    2024/4/12 "Woven Chair"
    Back in 2022/12/25 I was learning about the concept of baking high quality sculpting into normal maps. So I started to make a woven chair model to do normal map baking with. I sculpted one of the legs then got bored and switched projects. So 2 years later I eventually returned to the project, wanting to finish halfway done projects. And I sculpted the chair and completely forgot about the part where I wanted to bake it into a normal map. It also looked really bad so I tossed a shiny material on it to make it look better.
    2024/4/19 "Realistic Modern Room"
    I wanted to make a render that looked realistic. So I kept it simple and it came out sorta realistic.
    2024/5/24 "Procedural Building Maker"
    Blender nodes allow you to hypothetically do anything. Like people have made tree generators, vine generators, terrain generators, all within the same set of nodes. So I attempted to replicate the epicness of those generators, by making a building generator. And as I started to build out this node tree it started to get larger and larger. I also had to use many tricks like using the vertices on a circle to place objects in square. And by the end I managed to make a passable building generator.
    But the node tree behind the generator is horrible. Like I even put a couple more hours into trying to make it clean, but I couldn't make it better. I had taken Blenders geometry nodes to their limits, attempting to make something complicated enough that it was extremely difficult. Like if I had made this generator with python code, it would have been way easier. I might re-do this project but with some sort of code to blender integration. But that would be a new tool to learn, and I technically made something passable.
    2024/5/26 "Walking Animation"
    Animation is hard.
    Back in the way when I did lego stop motions, which may be the most entry level animation ever, It was still super difficult. Friend's who do animation will have 10s of hours go into 20 seconds of media. And I wanted to see if I could manage a walking animation. So I made a silly robot in blender, painted its bones, and got animating. It turns out animating walking is hard as like in the real world you kinda just walk but when animating it you have to think about how you walk and thats like something I had never even considered before. But I did technically make a walking animation. And its passable because its on a robot. If it where on a organic creature it would look horrible. Animation is hard.
    2024/9/2 "Earth Model"
    You can just download earth textures from Nasa. Like in one google search you can get 8k texture of earth collected by satellite. So I decided to download those textures, to attempt to make a accurate render of earth. I failed. That render is of earth, but not a accurate earth. The light for cities is far to bright, and the atmospheric effects are off. The mistake I made was continually layering new shaders instead of fixing old ones. Like instead of using the physically accurate emission, I just tossed another shader on.
    2024/11/24 "Flower Field Paint over"
    I was thinking of ways to stylize a image, like take a realistic image then make it more art like. And with graphics programming alot of that stylization comes from mathematical algorithms. But I got the idea to paint over a render it to stylize it. So I tried this idea and the result came out pretty well. Also it turns out drawing flowers is difficult.
