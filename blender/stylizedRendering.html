Stylized Rendering
3D Modeling and shading can be realistic, but it can also be very artful. And many times I have gotten ideas of non-realistic but still style-ful scenes to create.
2023/3/20 "Windows Background 1"
I wanted to personalize my computer, so I made a windows background. I used weight paints to control where the trees where, which may have been the first time I did that. As per usual the materials are the best looking part of it, with the models being passable. And the sky is just a image I composited in.
2023/7/28 "Windows Background 2"
The second windows background I made is very different. I had gotten quite interested into blenders node based materials. So I decided to make 45 of those materials for 1 image. And I really had to get creative to make more materials, like I discovered a number of new techniques to generate unique textures. Blenders nodes aren't really intended for generating texture like this, but I managed to push them to their limits.
2023/6/11 "Style Scene"
I wanted to make a non-realistic rendered scene, like stylized rendering. And I made something, but you don't need a design degree to say this image looks garbage. There ain't no color palette, no composition, no lighting, and most importantly no style.
2024/1/26 "Stylized Street Scene"
But multiple months later I returned, motivated to make style. Because I had gained a fragment of knowledge about stylized rendering. That good stylized rendering often modifies realistic rendering, instead of going on a completely new path. So I modified realistic rendering until it looked like this. And it came out baked.
I also made a explanation of the node tree behind the shader for a friend.
2024/11/24 "Flower Field Paint over"
I was thinking of ways to stylize a image, like take a realistic image then make it more art like. And with graphics programming alot of that stylization comes from mathematical algorithms. But I got the idea to paint over a render it to stylize it. So I tried this idea and the result came out pretty well. Also it turns out drawing flowers is difficult.
