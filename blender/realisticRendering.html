Realistic Rendering
In 2022 I played some videos with very realistic graphics, specifically Elden Ring and Red Dead Redemption 2. And that inspired me to create realistic models, and realistic renders.

But as it turns out, making anything look realistic is quiet difficult. So I would have to go on a full voyage over the course of years to make it happen.
2022/7/8 "Robot Tonk"
Elden Ring has 100s of unique enemy designs, and I wanted to try making one. It turns out, industry standard models are way out of my skill. As the enemy I made even when being very basic looks way lower quality then anything in Elden Ring. The biggest issue with it is the base surface is too flat, as I should have done some sort of sculpting. From this project I also learned about blending materials, which it turns out blender has poor support for and I had to build a node tree and make image masks myself.
2022/7/14 "Realistic Object"
This box is simple, but it's materials are not. I learnt about PBR material while texturing this, and it turns out light physics are quiet complicated. Like the small finger prints are part of the roughness channel, As in the real world finger print oil would effect the roughness. While in the past I probably would have put the finger print oil in the albedo channel. So this is a step in direction of being able to model materials accurately.
2022/7/22 "Reflective Room"
I learning about blenders Ray Tracing, and realized I could make infinity mirrors that would be impossible in real life. So after making a very reflective material, and changing the max number of allowed reflections, I was able to render the infinity mirror room.
2022/12/24 "Bottle"
This bottle is'nt particularly realistic, but while modeling it I learnt texture painting in blender. Which is a a sort of digital painting system, allowing drawing unique textures.
2024/4/12 "Woven Chair"
Back in 2022/12/25 I was learnt about baking high quality sculpted models into normal maps. Which allows for rendering super detailed models very efficiently. So I started to make this woven chair model to try normal map baking on.

On the right you can see the max quality chair with 850,000 triangles. And two chairs with significantly lower triangles, but similar qualities. The difference between the two chairs with baked normals, is in one I used a hand modeled approximation of the chair. And in the other I used a decimated version of the HD model, which helped preserve some detail, but also looks a bit too lumpy.
2024/4/19 "Realistic Modern Room"
I wanted to make a render that looked realistic. So I kept it simple and it came out sorta realistic.
2024/5/26 "Walking Animation"
Animation is hard.
Back in the way when I did lego stop motions, which may be the most entry level animation ever, It was still super difficult. Friend's who do animation will have 10s of hours go into 20 seconds of media. And I wanted to see if I could manage a walking animation. So I made a silly robot in blender, painted its bones, and got animating. It turns out animating walking is hard as like in the real world you kinda just walk but when animating it you have to think about how you walk and thats like something I had never even considered before. But I did technically make a walking animation. And its passable because its on a robot. If it where on a organic creature it would look horrible. Animation is hard.
2024/9/2 "Earth Model"
You can just download earth textures from Nasa. Like in one google search you can get 8k texture of earth collected by satellite. So I decided to download those textures, to attempt to make a accurate render of earth. I failed. That render is of earth, but not a accurate earth. The light for cities is far to bright, and the atmospheric effects are off. The mistake I made was continually layering new shaders instead of fixing old ones. Like instead of using the physically accurate emission, I just tossed another shader on.
2024/5/24 "Procedural Building Maker"
Blender nodes allow you to hypothetically do anything. Like people have made tree generators, vine generators, terrain generators, all within the same set of nodes. So I attempted to replicate the epicness of those generators, by making a building generator. And as I started to build out this node tree it started to get larger and larger. I also had to use many tricks like using the vertices on a circle to place objects in square. And by the end I managed to make a passable building generator.
But the node tree behind the generator is horrible. Like I even put a couple more hours into trying to make it clean, but I couldn't make it better. I had taken Blenders geometry nodes to their limits, attempting to make something complicated enough that it was extremely difficult. Like if I had made this generator with python code, it would have been way easier. I might re-do this project but with some sort of code to blender integration. But that would be a new tool to learn, and I technically made something passable.
